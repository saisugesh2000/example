Cards

A credit card allows the cardholder to borrow funds from the card issuer up to a certain credit limit to make purchases. Cardholders are required to repay the borrowed amount along with any accrued interest charges. A debit card is linked to the cardholder's bank account, allowing them to make purchases using funds directly from their account.

Core Banking

Core banking systems manage customer accounts, including savings accounts, current accounts, loans, and other financial products. They maintain detailed records of account balances, transactions, interest accruals, and account holder information.such as deposits, withdrawals, fund transfers, loan disbursements, and payments.
